# Dinner and a Show — Site Plan
Jonathan Patten - WDD 130
## Overview
### Purpose
The Dinner and a Show website serves as the central hub for the podcast and its growing community. It provides easy access to all episodes, host bios, episode guides, and listener resources. The site showcases and markets the podcast to new and returning audiences, turning passive listeners into engaged community members through exclusive content and direct connection with the four hosts.
### Audience
Our primary audience is college students who enjoy personality-driven entertainment podcasts. They are active on social media, appreciate authentic conversations, and look for a mix of humor, insight, and community connection. Secondary audiences include fans of individual hosts and listeners who discover the show through Spotify, Apple Podcasts, or word of mouth.

## Home Page
- Hero section with podcast logo, tagline ('Where Every Episode is a Main Course'), and a featured episode player embed.
- Highlight reel: rotating quotes from the podcast's greatest moments (e.g., 'That's the most unhinged thing I've ever heard. — Eva, Ep. 42').
- Meet the Hosts: four host profile cards with photo, name, and one-line bio linking to full Host Profiles page.
- Latest Episode: auto-updating card showing the most recent episode with title, date, and a 'Listen Now' button.
- Subscribe strip: icons and links for Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube, and RSS.
### Hosts Page
- Individual profile sections for Jonathan, Brayden, Eva, and Linnea.
- Each profile: headshot photo, bio, favorite episode picks, fun fact, and links to personal social media (Instagram, Twitter/X, TikTok).
- 'Host Commentary' feature — short exclusive audio or text commentary clips from each host.
### Episodes Page
Full episode library with search, filter by topic or season, and sort by date.
Each episode card includes: title, thumbnail, air date, guest name (if applicable), and short description.
